The Pigpen Cipher

The Pigpen Cipher is the most common monoalphabetic substitution cipher, a form of encryption that which utilizes the association of letters with images that would be characterized as individual letters in text. The set of images above with their associating letters is the most common variant of ciphers that use such a monoalphabetic form of encryption, although the name, Pigpen Cipher, may often be associated with a few other image based codes.

SCI-FI The Black Hole

On the journey to find a habitable bearing planet for your species, you and your space crew is hijacked by interdimensional pirates. The mischievous looters, having snuck relaxive sedatives into your bloodstream, therefore putting you to sleep, have sabotaged your ship to fly straight into the event horizon of a nearby black hole in hopes of entertainment. Additionally, all of your safety equipment have been deactivated. In order to turn the ship back on and redirect your ship, your team must manually open the door to the power room. Unfortunately, 3 safety deadlocks have been placed on the door. Your team is to find the codes placed by the troublesome pirates to save the crew.



This room requires 3 to 6 players.
